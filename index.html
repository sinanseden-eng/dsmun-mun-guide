üí° THIMUN Pro Tips

  • Jacket On: A delegate must have their jacket/blazer on anytime they stand to speak.
  • Respect the Chair: The Chair's rulings are final.
  • Stand Up: Always stand when speaking.
  • Be Diplomatic: Attack policies, not delegates.
  • One Thing at a Time: One resolution and one amendment at a time.
  • No Interruptions: Speakers cannot be interrupted except for audibility.
  • Voting is Sacred: No interruptions once voting begins except Point of Order related to the vote.

A Simple Guide to Writing a Resolution

(THIMUN Style)

What is a Resolution?

A resolution is a single, long sentence that proposes a solution to a problem. It is split into two main parts:

  • Preambulatory Clauses (The "WHY"): Explains background and context.
  • Operative Clauses (The "HOW"): The action plan.

Part 1: The Header

This is the "address" of your resolution. It must be 100% correct.

FORUM: General Assembly First Committee (Your committee)
QUESTION OF: The role of diamonds in fueling conflict... (Topic exactly as written)
SUBMITTED BY: Saint Vincent and the Grenadines (Main author)
CO-SUBMITTERS: Argentina, Australia, Canada, France... (Alphabetical)

Part 2: The Preamble (The "WHY")

This is the introduction. Remember:

  • Start with the full committee name in ALL CAPS (e.g., THE DISARMAMENT COMMISSION,)
  • Clause starters are italicized.
  • Every clause ends with a comma (,).

Here are common starters:

Aware of
Bearing in mind
Deeply concerned
Declaring
Emphasizing
Expressing its satisfaction
Guided by
Having examined
Keeping in mind
Noting with regret
Reaffirming
Recalling
Full Example Preamble ‚ñº
Aware of that 4% of diamonds produced are classified as "conflict or blood diamonds",

Recognizing the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme (KPCS) as an internationally recognized certification system,

Further recognizing that the KPCS is lacking a comprehensive system for monitoring where diamonds are mined, how they are mined and how proceeds are used,

Part 3: The Operative (The "HOW")

Remember:

  • Clauses are numbered (1, 2, 3...).
  • Clause starters are underlined.
  • Every clause ends with a semicolon (;), except the last which ends with a period (.)

Common operative starters:

Approves
Asks
Authorizes
Calls for
Urges
Requests
Encourages
Recommends
Operative Example & Sub-clauses ‚ñº
1. Calls for all relevant Member States to sign and ratify the KPCS to promote cooperation and awareness of trade;

2. Further calls for all relevant nations which are withholding signature... to request funds from IGOs such as IMF and World Bank;

3. Asks for the creation of the Collation for Stopping Conflict Diamonds (CSCD), which would work with Member States in order to stop illicit diamond transportation by:
a. monitoring who is buying and selling the diamonds
b. ensuring the methods are legal according to KPCS
c. sending submitted proof of diamond proceeds to ensure they are not funding armed conflicts
d. sending an observer team in the first year following ratification;

Annotated Sample Resolution

Full example (trimmed for readability) ‚Äî highlights and remarks included.

FORUM: Disarmament Sub-Commission 1
QUESTION OF: The role of diamonds in fueling conflict: breaking the link between the illicit transaction of rough diamonds and armed conflict
SUBMITTED BY: Saint Vincent and the Grenadines
CO-SUBMITTERS: Argentina, Australia, Canada, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Japan, Mexico, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, T√ºrkiye, United Kingdom, United States of America
The Header must be perfect. The FORUM and QUESTION OF must match the official agenda exactly. CO-SUBMITTERS must be listed in alphabetical order.
THE DISARMAMENT COMMISSION,

Noting that smuggling of illicit rough diamonds accounts for over 50% of the diamond trade and is estimated at 100 million USD per year,

Further noting illicit rough diamonds are used as "conflict or blood diamonds" to finance conflicts,

Aware that 4% of diamonds produced are classified as conflict diamonds which are enough to purchase weapons to destabilize regions,
Preambulatory Clauses define the problem, cite scale, and avoid repeating starters.
Recognizing the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme (KPCS), established in 2003,

1. Calls for all relevant Member States to sign and ratify the KPCS;

2. Further calls for nations withholding signature to request funds from IGOs;

3. Asks for the creation of the Collation for Stopping Conflict Diamonds (CSCD), which will:
a. monitor national diamond trades
b. ensure legality of mining methods
c. submit proof of proceeds to CSCD;
Operative clauses provide actions; sub-clauses use colons and lists. The last operative clause should end with a period.
Resolution Do's and Don'ts ‚ñº

Do...

  • Write the entire resolution as one sentence.
  • End preambulatory clauses with a comma.
  • End operative clauses with a semicolon; last clause ends with a period.
  • List co-submitters alphabetically.

Don't...

  • Use personal pronouns (I, we, our).
  • Repeat clause starters without 'Further'.
  • Have lists with a single item (a. requires b.).

How to Structure a Speech (60-90 Seconds)

                    

                        
1. Opening (10 sec): State your position clearly.

                        

                        
2. Key Points (40-60 sec): Give 2-3 strong arguments.

                        

                        
3. Closing (10 sec): Reinforce your position and call to action.
